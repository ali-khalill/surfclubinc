Official Bio

Hit-Boy —
The Story.

Chauncey Alexander Hollis Jr. (born May 21, 1987), known professionally as Hit-Boy, is a 3× Grammy Award-winning American record producer, rapper, and songwriter from Fontana, California. He is the founder and CEO of Surf Club Inc., a full-service label, publishing company, and management firm.

Hit-Boy began his career signing to Polow Da Don's Zone 4 / Interscope Records as an in-house producer, before joining Kanye West's GOOD Music in 2011. That same year, he produced "Niggas in Paris" for JAY-Z and Kanye West — a track that received Diamond certification from the RIAA and won two Grammy Awards for Best Rap Performance and Best Rap Song at the 55th Annual Grammy Awards.

Over the following decade, Hit-Boy's production appeared on chart-topping records by Travis Scott ("Sicko Mode," #1 Hot 100), Beyoncé ("Sorry," Lemonade), Nipsey Hussle ("Racks in the Middle" ft. Roddy Ricch, Grammy winner), Kendrick Lamar, ASAP Rocky, Nicki Minaj, Drake, and Lil Wayne, among many others.

Beginning in 2020, Hit-Boy executive produced six consecutive albums for Nas, starting with King's Disease — which gave Nas his first Grammy Award after a 30-year career. In 2024, he released GOLDFISH in collaboration with The Alchemist, accompanied by an original short film. Most recently, Hit-Boy provided additional production on Beyoncé's #1 single "TEXAS HOLD 'EM."

In 2026, Hit-Boy released BLOCK PARTY featuring Lefty Gunplay (February) and Yeast Talkin' Deluxe with Spank Nitti James (25 tracks, February 13) — continuing Surf Club Inc.'s prolific independent release cadence via EMPIRE and The Orchard.
