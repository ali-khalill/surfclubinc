The Origin

Built From
Waves.

A message on Myspace. A session at Record Plant. A career that would change hip-hop.

Chauncey Alexander Hollis Jr. — Hit-Boy — was born May 21, 1987, in Fontana, California. He began posting beats on Myspace as a teenager and received a message that changed everything: Polow Da Don. "Let's get this paper, pimp."

He signed to Zone 4 / Interscope as an in-house producer, then made his way to Kanye West's GOOD Music in 2011. That same year, he produced "Niggas in Paris" for Jay-Z and Kanye West — a track that peaked at #5 on the Billboard Hot 100, received Diamond certification (10M+ certified), and won two Grammy Awards. Jay-Z introduced Hit-Boy onstage at the Watch The Throne World Tour.

Over the next decade, Hit-Boy's production appeared on records by Travis Scott, Beyoncé, Kendrick Lamar, Nipsey Hussle, Roddy Ricch, Nicki Minaj, Drake, ASAP Rocky, Lil Wayne, and dozens more. In 2020, he executive produced Nas's King's Disease — giving Nas his first Grammy Award after a 30-year career — beginning a six-album run together.

Today, Surf Club Inc. is a full-service label, publishing company, and management firm — distributed through EMPIRE and The Orchard, with a publishing joint venture through EMPIRE Publishing. Still independent. Still building from the Inland Empire.
